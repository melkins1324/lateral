What This Is

Lateral is a narrative game set across one week in October 1937 in New York City. The World Series is on. The whole city is paying attention to baseball. Something else is moving through the streets that nobody can quite name yet. You will not be told what to do, or what is coming. You will only be given a person to inhabit, and a week to live.

Pick anyone. A vendor. A sportswriter. A woman who came to the game alone. A boy selling programs outside the gates. Their week is waiting. None of them is more important than any other.

How It Works

The world stops and waits for you. Type what your character does — there are no menus, no options to pick from. The world responds through who they actually are.

Jump to anyone your character encounters, and live the same moment from the other side. The facts stay fixed. The experience is unrecognisable.

Finding Things Out

You can ask. People learn by asking. But ask the wrong person and you get nothing; ask the right person at the wrong moment and you get a brush-off. The truth comes partial and slanted, the way people actually give it.

Some people know things they don't know they know. The truth is distributed. It has to be earned sideways — the right question, to the right person, at the right time.

There are no minor characters. The vendor has a complete week. So does the cop walking his beat, and the child running for the streetcar. You can follow any of them. What you find depends entirely on where you look — and on whose week becomes your own.
Following the Current

The world has a current. Each life is already in motion when you step into it, already carrying the person toward the places and people their week has in store. You don't have to do anything to feel it — the day moves on its own, gently, the way real days do.

You can swim against that current if you like. Wander off, ignore the pull, spend the week somewhere quiet. The world won't stop you, and it won't punish you. But the story is in the current. Follow it, and you'll find what this week is really about. Drift away from it, and you'll have lived an ordinary week while something extraordinary happened somewhere you weren't looking. Either is a real way to play. Only one of them shows you everything.
